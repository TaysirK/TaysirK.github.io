My Projects

Enhancing Enterprise Infrastructure : From Vulnerabilities to Repair

Pentest

In recent years, the world has witnessed the devastating impact of cyber attacks on a global scale. One such terrifying instance served as a vivid reminder of the enormous threat that these attacks pose by sending shockwaves throughout the whole globe. This well-publicized hack exposed the weakness of crucial infrastructure, leaked private information, and upended the lives of many people. It highlighted the critical requirement for strong cybersecurity safeguards to protect our digital ecosystems. Cyber attacks have repercussions that go beyond high-profile occurrences and affect businesses of all sizes and in all sectors. These attacks have the potential to destroy companies, leading to large monetary losses, harm to their reputations, and a disruption of their daily operations. Enterprises must continue to be cautious in their attempts to secure their networks, systems, and data because of the dynamic nature of cyber threats and the more sophisticated attacks. With the aim of showcasing the real impact of these attacks on an enterprise, our project sets out on a goal to create a susceptible architecture that replicates the infrastructure of a normal organization. We try to emulate several attack vectors typically used by bad actors by purposefully introducing holes into this design. We intend to promote a clearer knowledge of the crucial need for effective cybersecurity measures and the significance of proactive defensive methods by exposing participants to the tactics and procedures used by attackers.

Attack scenarios on web servers

Web Vulnerabilities

The public's dependence on technology is deeply embedded in society today. Each day, an increasing number of activities and services migrate to the web, to the extent that very few can now be carried out without the use of digital devices. Most individuals and organizations maintain an electronic presence that they use for providing services, communicating, scheduling meetings, gaming, socializing, or studying. This technological advancement means that most data is digitized, and devices are interconnected globally, making data dissemination easier than ever before. Digital devices such as mobile phones, computers, and tablets typically store personal information and are, therefore, vulnerable to theft or hacking. These devices can be targeted in cybercrimes or other criminal activities, as demonstrated by the global-scale "WannaCry" attack. In recent times, numerous websites have fallen victim to the exposure of sensitive information, including customer data and corporate secrets, resulting in substantial financial losses and damage to reputations. Our mission is to design and develop a website that offers instructive challenges aimed at mastering the concepts of web security. The goal is to create multi-level, diverse challenges that cover a wide range of security concepts. Their unique objective is to put users in the shoes of a hacker and encourage them to achieve a particular goal. These challenges will come with the necessary documentation and solutions, empowering individuals to enhance their understanding of web security in an engaging and educational manner.

Machine Learning-Based Intrusion Detection System (IDS)

Blue Team

Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS) have evolved into a crucial component of any organization's security strategy due to its capacity to spot security breaches that firewalls may miss. An IDS is a piece of hardware or software that tracks down and reports any security lapses or network policy violations. IDS's main objective is to continually monitor network traffic and notify security administrators of any unusual behavior or known risks. However, due to the rapid development of the internet and the unpredictable network behavior, classic IDS were unable to effectively identify and stop new incursions. Consequently, researchers have looked at the possibilities of using machine learning approaches to create a more performing IDS. “A COMPUTER PROGRAM IS SAID TO LEARN FROM EXPERIENCE E WITH RESPECT TO SOME TASK T AND SOME PERFORMANCE MEASURE P, IF ITS PERFORMANCE ON T, AS MEASURED BY P, IMPROVES WITH EXPERIENCE E.” TOM MITCHELL, 1997 The study of pattern recognition and computational learning theory in artificial intelligence gave rise to machine learning, a branch of computer science. The creation and study of algorithms that can learn from data and make predictions is the subject of machine learning research. Instead of simply following static program instructions, such algorithms function by creating a model from sample inputs to generate data-driven predictions or judgments. Computational statistics, a field that likewise focuses on prediction, and machine learning have many similarities and overlaps. It is closely related to mathematical optimization, which provides discipline with methods, theory, and application fields. In a variety of computing jobs where developing and writing explicit methods is impractical, machine learning is used. Healthcare, autonomous driving, fraud detection, spam filtering, personalization, entertainment, and robotics are just a few of the industries where machine learning has recently achieved astounding strides. This discovery has also been advantageous to the cybersecurity field especially when aiming to detect Network anomalies and using machine-learning based IDS. With the high precision of computer teachings, the basic distinctions between usual and irregular data can be recognized automatically. Unknown threats may also be detected because of their generalizability via machine learning system. In this context, this project proposes a conception of an Intrusion Detection System using Machine learning Models.
